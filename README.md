- # Retail Prices of Fruits and Vegetables 2020 & 2022
A data visualization project analyzing average retail prices of fruits 
and vegetables across product types (fresh, dried, frozen, canned, juice) 
using USDA retail scanner data from 2020 and 2022.
- # Problem Statement
Consumers and retailers lack clear visibility into how fruit and vegetable 
prices vary by product type. This project identifies which categories are 
most and least affordable to support smarter buying and stocking decisions.
- ## Tools Used
- Tableau (Data cleaning, visualization, dashboard)
- Tableau Data Interpreter (Data preparation)
 - ## Dataset
- Source: USDA Economic Research Service
- Years: 2020 and 2022
- Fields: Product name, type (fresh/dried/frozen/canned/juice),
  average retail price per unit
   - ## Key Findings
- Dried fruits were the most expensive product type in 2020,
  with Mangoes, Figs, and Apricots commanding the highest 
  average retail prices
- Raspberries and Blackberries were the priciest fresh fruits 
  in 2022, averaging ~$10-11 per unit
- Asparagus was the most expensive vegetable in 2022, 
  reaching an average retail price of ~$13
- Frozen fruits were consistently cheaper than their 
  fresh equivalents across all fruit categories
- Dried vegetables ranked as the most expensive vegetable 
  type while canned vegetables were the most affordable
- Budget-friendly fruits included Watermelon, Bananas, 
  and Pineapple — all priced under $2 on average
  - ## Visualizations Built
- Stacked bar chart — fruit price comparison by type (fresh vs frozen 2022)
- Tree map — average price distribution across all product types (2020)
- Horizontal bar chart — vegetable price comparison (2022)
- Line chart — vegetable price trends by type (2022)
- Bullet chart — fruit prices by type (2020)
